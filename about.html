About Crop Circle Watch

Crop Circle Watch is a growing, systematically maintained log of newly reported crop circle formations — a precise record of when and where each one appears, with source links and original footage credited to the researchers and photographers documenting them on the ground. It launched in June 2026 and adds new formations the day they surface, building an open, verifiable timeline of a phenomenon that has recurred in fields around the world for decades.

Why it's here

Whatever you make of crop circles — human artistry, unexplained anomaly, or something in between — the formations are real, and they keep arriving, season after season. Most in this record remain formally unclassified; a documented share are confirmed human-made, and a smaller, closely studied set exhibit measurable traits in the crop that known construction methods have not reproduced. Crop Circle Watch doesn't argue the question — it keeps the evidence: an honest, growing archive, every entry traceable to the people doing the fieldwork, so the pattern can be seen clearly and the truth has somewhere to accumulate.
