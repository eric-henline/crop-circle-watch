About Crop Circle Watch

cereology /ˌsɪərɪˈɒlədʒɪ/ n. the study or investigation of crop circles

Crop Circle Watch is a small, standing log of newly reported crop circle formations — not a theory about what makes them, just a careful record of when and where a new one shows up, with a source link and footage for each. It launched in June 2026 as a side project, separate from the larger research and book work happening elsewhere in the same family of projects.

How it works

Once a day, an automated scan checks recent reports from crop-circle photography and research sites, filters out anything that's just old news resurfacing, and adds genuinely new formations to the log with a verified date, location, and source link. Nothing here is generated or guessed — every entry traces back to a real report, and every video is the original footage from the photographer or research group that found it. A person can always edit, correct, or add entries by hand; the automation only ever adds to the record, it never rewrites it.

Why it's here

Whatever you make of crop circles — hoax, art, mystery, all three — new ones really do appear in fields most years, and the people documenting them rarely get much attention beyond a small, dedicated community. This is a quiet way of keeping watch: a running record of an odd, very real phenomenon, kept honest by linking back to the people doing the actual fieldwork.
